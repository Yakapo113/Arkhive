Reading this

Planning model, not financial advice. Everything is in today's dollars (inflation-adjusted). Contributions stop when you retire; withdrawals are taxed by account type; RMDs are forced from 401(k)/IRA at your RMD age and reinvested if unspent. Real-estate & business assets grow and are liquidated at their sell age (after cap-gains tax), with proceeds reinvested. Social Security and other income are treated as after-tax. Withdrawals from 401(k)/IRA/Roth before age 59½ include the 10% IRS early-withdrawal penalty — so an early retiree really does need a taxable/cash bridge. Two risk engines: History replays every real market sequence since 1928 (S&P 500 + Treasuries + CPI, Damodaran data) using your stock/bond split; Monte Carlo draws random returns from your return & volatility. Real estate/business use their own growth rate, not the market series. Verify your Social Security estimate at ssa.gov.
