How to Use This Real Estate Investment Calculator

This free real estate investment calculator helps you size up a rental property in seconds. Enter the purchase price, financing terms, expected rent, and operating costs, and it instantly estimates the numbers investors use to judge a deal — cap rate, cash-on-cash return, monthly cash flow, DSCR, and long-term ROI — accounting for taxes, depreciation, vacancy, and appreciation across your hold period. Start with the color-coded Quick Deal Screen for a fast gut check, then open the Advanced simulator for year-by-year detail.

Key Metrics Explained

1% Rule. A fast first screen — monthly rent divided by purchase price. At or above 1% is a promising sign the rent is high enough relative to price; below about 0.8% often points to thin cash flow. It's a filter, not a final verdict.

Cap Rate (capitalization rate). Net operating income divided by purchase price. It measures the property's return before financing, so you can compare deals no matter how each one is funded. As a rough guide, 8%+ is strong, 5–8% is moderate, and under 5% is tight — though what counts as "good" varies widely by market.

Cash-on-Cash Return. Annual pre-tax cash flow divided by the actual cash you invest (down payment plus closing costs). This is your real return on the money out of your own pocket. Many investors target 8–10% or more.

Monthly Cash Flow. What's left each month after the mortgage and every operating expense. Positive cash flow means the property pays you to own it; negative means it costs you money every month you hold it.

DSCR (debt service coverage ratio). Net operating income divided by annual mortgage payments — the number lenders scrutinize most. A DSCR of 1.0 means the property exactly covers its own debt; most lenders want 1.25+ for a safety margin. Below 1.0, the rent doesn't cover the loan.

GRM (gross rent multiplier). Purchase price divided by annual gross rent — a quick way to rank similar listings, where lower is generally better. Handy for comparing comps, less useful as a standalone buy/pass signal.

ROI and Annualized ROI (CAGR). Your total profit over the hold period — cash flow plus loan paydown plus appreciation, minus taxes and selling costs — measured against your initial investment. Annualized ROI expresses that as an average compounded yearly return.

Good to Know

Results are estimates for screening deals, not financial or tax advice. Tax figures use simplified assumptions (including 27.5-year residential depreciation and the rates you enter), and real returns depend on your specific market, loan terms, and management. Always confirm the numbers with your lender and accountant, and do your own due diligence, before making an offer.
